# ABF Product Philosophy
*Always Be Flirting - Core Product Vision*

## The Vision

ABF exists to help couples:
1. **Introduce each partner to each other** in ways they may not be accustomed to
2. **Grow a relationship** they are both active in and responsible for
3. **Build with pride and balance** - celebrating the entity they create together

We are not building a couples messaging app. We are not building a task manager for relationships. We are building a **relationship development platform** that helps partners understand each other deeply, adapt meaningfully, and ultimately tend to their relationship as a living thing they nurture together.

---

## The Three-Stage Journey

### Stage 1: Introduction & Discovery
**Focus:** Understanding individual differences
**Question:** "Who are you? Who am I?"
**Outcome:** Partners learn each other's communication styles, conflict patterns, emotional needs, values, affection preferences, and partnership approaches

**What we build:**
- 18-question onboarding that reveals core dimensions
- Compatibility scoring that shows alignment and differences
- Partner insights that translate answers into understanding

**Mindset shift:** "I thought everyone was like me" → "Oh, you're wired differently!"

---

### Stage 2: Adaptation & Validation
**Focus:** Individual effort toward partner's needs
**Question:** "How do I show up for YOU?"
**Outcome:** Partners learn to meet each other where they are, even when it's outside their comfort zone

**What we build:**
- Targeted suggestions based on partner's needs
- Effort tracking (private, non-judgmental)
- Positive validation loops (gratitude, recognition)
- Growth area identification with compassionate framing

**Mindset shift:** "I show love my way" → "I show love in ways that land for you"

---

### Stage 3: Joint Ownership & Maturity
**Focus:** The relationship as its own entity
**Question:** "How is OUR relationship doing?"
**Outcome:** Partners shift from "I do this for you" to "We do this for US"

**What we build:**
- Relationship health metrics (not just compatibility scores)
- Joint rituals and shared activities
- Collective reflection and goal-setting
- Celebration of the relationship itself

**Mindset shift:** "I'm working on us" → "We're raising our relationship together"

---

## The Core Law

**Every feature must:**

1. **Address a core relationship need**
   - Communication
   - Conflict Resolution
   - Emotional Intimacy & Trust
   - Values & Future Goals
   - Affection & Quality Time
   - Support & Partnership

2. **Create positive validation loops**
   - Partner A's effort is recognized by Partner B
   - Recognition feels rewarding, not obligatory
   - No feature creates negative confrontation
   - Validation builds momentum, not resentment

3. **Build toward joint ownership**
   - Early: Individual actions toward partner
   - Middle: Coordinated adaptation
   - Mature: Joint investment in the relationship

**If a feature doesn't meet at least one of these criteria, we don't build it.**

## Guiding Principles

### Principle 1: Never Create Conflict
**The problem:**
Direct tracking of compliance creates shame and blame.
- "Did your partner do X?" → They say no → Partner feels attacked
- "You didn't try this week" → Shame spiral
- "Your partner rated you 2/10" → Fight starter

**Our approach:**
- Frame everything as gratitude and celebration
- Decouple action from judgment
- Celebrate wins, gently guide on misses
- Private self-accountability, public recognition only
- **Test:** If a prompt could generate a response that would hurt to see, don't ask it

---

### Principle 2: Recognize Effort Differently
**The problem:**
Treating all actions equally ignores difficulty.
- Partner A (naturally affectionate) gives hug → Easy
- Partner B (touch-averse) gives hug → Huge effort
- Same action, different meaning

**Our approach:**
- Acknowledge starting points openly
- "Growth areas" highlight where effort is harder
- System weighs difficulty (2x multiplier for stretch behaviors)
- Teach Partner A: "When Partner B does this, it means more"
- Reward Partner B: "This is hard for you, and that's valuable"

---

### Principle 3: Meet in the Middle
**The problem:**
"Be more like your partner" creates inauthenticity.
- Partner needs constant affection
- You're not wired that way
- Forced compliance feels fake

**Our approach:**
- Show the gap clearly
- Suggest compromise: "Try 3 intentional moments" (not constant)
- Acknowledge: "This stretches you but honors your style"
- Frame: "Small gestures matter more when they're outside your comfort zone"
- Goal: Sustainable adaptation, not personality change

---

### Principle 4: Surface Needs, Not Compliance
**The problem:**
Low-hanging fruit (flirts, date nights) without addressing core needs.
- 100 messages sent
- 50 dates planned
- Still can't handle conflict or communicate needs
- Health meter says 95% (but it's a lie)

**Our approach:**
- Every action maps to a core category
- Flirts? Only suggest ones aligned with their love language
- Date nights? Include reflection on communication/conflict patterns
- Daily check-ins? Rotate through all 6 core needs
- Health meter reflects REAL progress on core dimensions

---

### Principle 5: Progress Over Perfection
**The problem:**
All-or-nothing thinking kills motivation.
- Missed a week → "I'm failing"
- Partner didn't notice → "What's the point?"
- Conflict happened → "We're broken"

**Our approach:**
- Changes take weeks to land - normalize this
- "Not this week (that's okay!)" as an option
- Consistency matters more than perfection
- Celebrate attempts, not just successes
- Reframe conflict: "Every couple has these - you're learning to navigate yours"

---

### Principle 6: The Relationship is the Third Entity
**The problem:**
Focus stays on individuals competing for needs met.
- "What I need vs. what you need"
- Scorekeeping and transactional thinking
- "I did this, so you should do that"

**Our approach:**
- Early: Individual differences are revealed
- Middle: Individual adaptations are validated
- Mature: Joint investment in relationship health
- Metaphor: "You're raising a relationship together"
- Question shifts: "What does OUR relationship need right now?"
- Celebrate: "Look how strong you've made this relationship!"

## Feature Decision Framework

Before building any feature, answer these questions:

### 1. Core Need
**Which core category does this address?**
- [ ] Communication
- [ ] Conflict Resolution
- [ ] Emotional Intimacy & Trust
- [ ] Values & Future Goals
- [ ] Affection & Quality Time
- [ ] Support & Partnership

If none: Don't build it.

### 2. Validation
**How does this create positive feedback?**
- Does Partner A see Partner B's effort?
- Is recognition non-confrontational?
- Can partners feel good even if it's imperfect?

If it creates shame/blame: Redesign it.

### 3. Stage Alignment
**What stage is this feature for?**
- Stage 1 (Discovery): Shows differences
- Stage 2 (Adaptation): Guides individual effort
- Stage 3 (Joint): Builds shared ownership

If unclear: Clarify the purpose.

### 4. Sustainability
**Can couples do this long-term?**
- Is it overwhelming? (Too much, too fast)
- Is it superficial? (Busy work without meaning)
- Is it authentic? (Feels genuine, not forced)

If not sustainable: Simplify or remove.

- Partner insights engagement

**Anti-metrics (things we DON'T optimize for):**

- Raw engagement time

## Monetization Strategy

ABF will generate revenue through three primary channels:
1. **Subscription Revenue** - Core recurring revenue from couples
2. **Affiliate Revenue** - Commission from bookings and purchases
3. **Strategic Partnerships** - B2B deals with experience providers

### 5. Engagement & Retention Strategy

#### Stickiness Factors
**What keeps couples coming back:**
1. **Daily ritual** - Check-in becomes part of routine
2. **Sunk cost** - Timeline memories they don't want to lose
3. **Partner engagement** - Both need to participate
4. **AI relationship** - Coach knows their history
5. **Progress visibility** - Health meter shows growth

#### Rewards System
**Non-monetary gamification (gentle):**
- Streak celebrations (7 days, 30 days, 100 days)
- Milestone badges (relationship anniversaries)
- Hidden achievements (unlocked naturally)
- "Memory unlocked" when old check-ins resurface
- Partner appreciation prompts

**Monetary rewards (Couples+ tier):**
- Monthly discount codes for date experiences
- Anniversary gift vouchers
- Exclusive partner deals

#### Churn Prevention
**Signals we watch:**
- Check-in frequency dropping
- AI coach usage declining
- One partner more active than other
- No timeline additions in 30 days

**Interventions:**
- Gentle re-engagement nudges (not nagging)
- "We miss you" from AI coach
- Partner notification: "Haven't seen [name] in a while"
- Offer pause instead of cancel
- Exit survey with honest feedback
